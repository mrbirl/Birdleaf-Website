What are customer personas?

August 16, 2015 0

When it comes to your marketing efforts and deciding on what channels you should focus on it is really important to have a clear picture of your customer personas. These are fictional, generalized representations of your ‘ideal customers’ designed to help you understand your customers and potential customers better.

These cluster of customers should exhibit similar behavioural patterns in their purchasing decisions, use of technology or products, customer service preferences, lifestyle choices, and the like. Therefore how you market to each persona can change drastically depending on the customer segment makeup.

Customer personas are a semi-fictional representation of your ideal customer based on market research and real data about your existing customers.

Hubspot

The basic customer persona template

Creating your customer personas is quite straightforward, as regardless of your business there are a few common elements that should be included for a customer persona to be as effective as possible.

A lot of these templates include the same basic information like who the person is, what problems they have, and how to acquire these customers. Here is a quick overview on what you should include in your customer persona template:

Who?

Here you’re summing up common personal and professional demographics. These data points can be very useful when advertising as on a lot of ad networks you can set a lot of demographical filters to target people that match this demographical criteria.

  • Basic demographics
    • Age
    • Gender
    • Location
    • Family
  • Professional demographics
    • Job Title
    • Industry
    • Company Size
    • Company Revenue

 

What?

Understanding this part is key in empathizing with the customer. This relates to more specific traits like their personality, values, interests, and lifestyles. The basic demographics provide you with the ‘who’, whereas the Psychographics provide you with the ‘what’.

  • Problems
    • What problems does the customer have that you can solve?
  • Goals
    • What is their main goal motivating them to look for a solution?
  • Values
    • What’s the customers expectations of your product?
    • What are their objections during the buying process?

 

Why?

This section of the customer persona is all about your marketing message and how you will acquire new customers that fit the specific customer persona. For each customer persona you have how you explain your product should change along with the route to these customers.

  • Product Benefit
    • What benefit does your product offer the customer?
  • Marketing
    • How will you describe your product?
    • What routes will you use to reach these customers?

 

To be successful at creating customer personas you need to accurately define who the person is, what their problems and goals are, and how you will explain your marketing message.

Why You Should Have Customer Personas

At a high level, developing strong customer personas will result in more efficient and more cost effective marketing. They allow you to clearly segment your target audience into different groups meaning you can personalize and tailor your marketing for your different segments. For example, by segmenting your customer base into three groups you can send tailored content to each group meaning the content is more relevant and actionable for the end user leading to a better customer experience.

It also allows you, the marketer, to build a stronger brand by conveying stronger more relevant messaging and increase your return on investment on your marketing spend by focusing on the channels that will work in acquiring new ‘ideal customers’.

The easiest and most accurate source of information for your customer personas should be your existing customer base. Based on your current customers and what you know about them see if you can fill in data points needed for a rich customer persona. If you can’t fill out everything then the quickest way to fill in the gaps is by surveying or interviewing a random selection of your customers.

When creating your customer personas I suggest starting out with a small amount, like 3 or 4. Some companies can have more than 10 different customer personas whereas some companies may only have 3, this really just depends on the nature of your business and your target audience. Spending time on creating these customer personas will help you get a better understanding of your target audience meaning you’ll be better equipped to hit them with more relevant content. An all round win!
