The 10 best email marketing and newsletter apps

November 25, 2015 0

All this talk and debate about emails death is greatly exaggerated. A recent study concluded that email is almost 40 times better at acquiring new customers than Facebook, Twitter and Google Adwords.

With that in mind, email marketing should play a heavy role in your overall marketing strategy! In this post we’ll look at some of the best email marketing and newsletter apps to help you kick start your email marketing.

All the email marketing apps we mention are capable of sending great newsletters. A lot of the apps are a lot more feature rich than others so it depends what approach you want to take with your email marketing.

Factors to consider

When deciding on which email marketing app you should be purchase to run your campaigns there are 5 key factors to consider. Some factors will be more important than others depending on your business but it is worth keeping the importance of each factor to your business in mind as you read through this post.

  • Price
  • Reporting & Analytics
  • Segmentation
  • Customization
  • Automation

Below are the best apps that send email newsletters (in no particular order). For each app you’ll find a screenshot, a brief review, and pricing information.

1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is one of the most popular email marketing apps. It is incredibly simple and intuitive to use and it has tonnes of really slick pre-designed templates.  MailChimp offers everything from a drag-and-drop email editor to detailed subscriber profiles that track your contacts interactions with your emails, and more.

MailChimp allows you to easily customize your email campaigns for recipients with merge-tags. If you want to grab your reader’s attention by including their first name in the subject line (personalization is proven to be an effective marketing tactic), simple add the merge tag *|FNAME|*to the subject line. MailChimp also offers really simple segmentation. You can set certain criteria a subscriber must meet before sending them a tailored message. For example, for every subscriber whose location is New York send them the “New York Flash Sale” email.

Pricing: MailChimp offer a free plan that allows allows you have up to 2,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. From there they charge based on usage starting at $10 per month for unlimited emails to 500 subscribers.

 

2. AWeber

AWeber is another great option for those that are new to email marketing. All you need to do is simply choose a template (mobile responsive) and input your content. So no faffing about with design. However, for anyone who wants to customize the emails they send to their customers, this might not be their preferred way of working. There are a huge amount of templates to choose from (over 700 in fact) or you can pay a premium to have a template designed for you (starting from $29).

If you want to really make the most of your campaigns and generate a high ROI, learning to manage your subscribers effectively is essential. AWeber allows you to do a really good job at this. You can create heavily customized submission forms with up to 25 fields. Using this data it is really simple to segment your subscribers into different groups ensuring you provide more personalized content for the reader.

Pricing: AWeber offers a free fully functional 30-day trial. After this prices start at $19 per month for up to 500 subscribers and unlimited emails.

3. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign has one of the most comprehensive offerings on this list. Their product combines a full CRM and marketing automation suite along with its email newsletter tool meaning it’s not just about emails. It is really a platform to allow you to grow your business, manage your leads and your clients too.

ActiveCampaign provides a simple tool to create customized sign-up forms that you can display on your website and Facebook page to help you build your subscriber list. For your emails you have a full-featured email editor. You can drag-and-drop email elements, design them the way you want, and quickly send your finished newsletters off to your subscribers.

The best bit of ActiveCampaign is its campaign automation. They offer a variety of tools you can use to target your customers with timely messages and personalized content without spending every waking hour managing your campaigns.

Pricing: ActiveCampaign offer a 14-day trial. After this the entry level package is $9 per month for up to 500 subscribers and unlimited emails.

4. GetResponse

With over 53% of emails opened on mobile, GetResponse is a great option for those that put mobile first. It’s biggest strength is its responsive email design functionality that automatically adjusts your newsletter’s template so if a user is reading it on a smartphone, the layout and fonts will be automatically optimised for it. A lot of the competing platforms have added this functionality recently, but GetResponse is one of the only services that offers live mobile-previews.

GetResponse offers one of the most comprehensive reporting and analytics tools. Aside from the basics of open rates and clickthroughs they offer ‘one click segmentation’ and ’email ROI’ to name a few, meaning tracking important metrics for your business is a breeze. One other cool feature that GetResponse offers is a landing page creator to collect more leads.

Pricing: GetResponse offer a free 30-day trial. After this pricing starts at $15 for up to 1000 contacts and unlimited emails.

5. Constant Contact

ConstantContact offer a really slick drag and drop editor making it really seamless to customize one of their templates. They also offer a lot of extras that you can include in your emails such as polls, surveys, videos and more.

You can create comprehensive reports on your email campaigns to show number of opt-outs, complaints, bounces, forwards and click-throughs. The social-share tool allows you to easily post and track your emails on all your social networks. The social stats reporting tools clearly illustrate the number of shares, likes, tweets and more that your messages receive. You can also see the number of total social shares and page views your email campaign has generated.

Pricing: ConstantContact offer a 60-day free trial. After  this plans start at $20 per month for up to 500 subscribers and unlimited emails.

6. TinyLetter

If you’re looking for a no frills and super basic app then TinyLetter is for you! It’s ridiculously simple to use and has pretty much no features. It simply lets you create simple plain-text  newsletters and basic custom landing pages allowing you to collect email addresses.

TinyLetter is run by MailChimp and they will only allow you add up to 5,000 subscribers. After that you’ll need a more feature rich platform like MailChimp.

Pricing: Absolutely Free!

7. Emma

Emma‘s email app is well known for its simplicity and killer design. They provide you with a really slick drag and drop email editor and a lot of nice templates. One really interesting thing about Emma is they offer a concierge service to design and write your emails for you as well as setting up drip campaigns. So if you were looking on hiring a designer to craft your email campaigns then you should look no further than Emma as they know what works for each use case.

The also offer smart audience segmentation with unlimited groups and contact fields and simple automation workflows that keep your subscribers engaged.

Pricing: Emma offer a 30-day free-trial after which pricing starts at $49 per month for up to 2,500 subscribers and unlimited emails.

 

8. Mailjet

Mailjet allows you to send marketing and transactional email from one account to optimize your customer’s email experience. In addition to their send API, Mailjet includes a simple template designer where you can design responsive emails  templates on the fly without the help of your developer.

The big selling point for Mailjet is its powerful list segmentation where you can segment your subscribers based on preferences, behaviours and characteristics. Their  send API can be leveraged to constantly push fresh contact data from any source meaning the segmentation possibilities are endless!

Pricing: Mailjet offer a free 30-day trial and plans start from $7.49 per month for 30,000 emails.

9. Campaign Monitor

While using templates for your email marketing has many benefits, they can start to look a bit generic and have less impact. Campaign Monitor combats this by making it as easy as possible to code your own templates. You can import your own HTML templates by using Campaign Monitor’s tags to make sure you’re able to use its integrated template editor to edit and personalize content in future. That being said they do offer some nice templates that you can use and customize off the bat.

Campaign Monitor generates actual screens of your email design in popular email clients like Gmail, Hotmail, Yahoo and many more. This allows you to get your campaigns displaying properly in all email clients.

Pricing: Campaign Monitor have a free trial, but you can’t send any emails. Pricing starts at $9 per month for up to 500 contacts and 2,500 emails.

10. Freshmail

When you go to build your first newsletter in FreshMail the most noticable thing is the amount of customization you have. The FreshMail Designer is drag and drop and they offer very slick ready-to-use email templates. They offer a hugh amount of graphics so you can play around with lots of different designs.

One thing that is very notable from their editor is the ability to create emails that look like really nice product demo pages. Ideal for product launches and announcements. On top of their editor the offer good analytics and drip campaigns.

Pricing: FreshMail offer a free account for up to 500 subscribers and unlimted emails. Pricing then starts at $15 per month for up to 1000 subscribers.

Takeaways

And there we have it, that concludes our list of the 10 best email marketing and newsletter apps! From ridiculously simple email marketing apps to full-blown marketing automation apps there should be at least one that fits the bill for your business!

Pretty much all of the apps on the list offer a free trial allowing you to get a good overview of the product by creating an email campaign. Even if you only send a test one to your own email address.
