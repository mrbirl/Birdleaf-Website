Rapportive Alternatives That Don’t Suck

April 23, 2015 0

Are you one of those people who used Rapportive to find email addresses or information about the people you’re talking to? The Rapportive of old is very much dead and buried and while it still exists and you can still use it, all of thefunctionality that drew users to it in the first place is now unfortunately gone. Fortunately for all its fans a lot of good alternatives have popped up since LinkedIn neutered the once awesome tool.

FullContact

FullContact is an awesome replacement for Rapportive! Similar to Rapportive, users are able to type in or hover over a new email address in order to see detailed contact information in the sidebar of your inbox, including things like their name, location, Job Title, Company , recent tweets, and links to their social networks. It even goes a step further than the Rapportive of old by displaying rich company information. You’ll be able to see things like the company’s founding date, company size, phone number, address and more.

FullContact also allows you to make notes and tag people. The only caveat with this feature is after 5,000 contacts , you’ll have to upgrade to a premium account, but I’m guessing that having 5,000 contacts is a good problem to have so shelling out the $9.99 per month won’t be too big an issue.


FullContact(Free) | Availability Google Chrome & iOS

Vibe

VibeApp is also a very slick-looking alternative to Rapportive and it pulls in a lot of data. Like the rest of the competitors Rapportive throne VibeApp pulls up detailed contact information, links to their social profiles, organizations they’ve worked for, and associated websites in the sidebar of your inbox. It also pulls out topics or keywords that best describes the person but having tested it with a few email addresses its a bit hit and miss.

There are a few key differentiators between VibeApp and those trying to replace Rapportive. Firstly, it isn’t restricted to your inbox, it will work across your browser anywhere that an email address is displayed. For example, if you see someone’s email address on LinkedIn all you have to do is hover your mouse over it and it will show you all the other social accounts associated with that email address. Secondly, it’s not limited to a single email address in a message.

Vibe(Free) | Availability Google Chrome, Firefox, Mac & iOS

Sidekick

Sidekick by Hubspot is a very need alternative to Rapportive giving you a wealth of contact insight in your inbox. It has all the functionality of Rapportive and a lot, lot more! In the sidebar of your inbox you’ll see your contacts social profiles,  all their recent tweets, location, and their professional history. Now for the more part. Sidekick will show you your email history with that contact. It tracks and analyzes the behaviors of the person that receives your email so you can see exactly what they did with it.

Now for the more part! Sidekick will also show you your email history with that contact. It tracks and analyzes the behaviors of the person that receives your email so you can see exactly what they did with it. How many times your email has been opened, if they clicked on the link in the email, what device they read your email on. Really useful insights so you can determine  the effectiveness of your email strategy. For example, if a potential client is reading your emails on their phone, you will want to tailor your content to be as short and concise as possible whereas if they’re on their laptop you may want to go into a bit more depth.

Sidekick (Free) | Availability Google Chrome

Discover.ly

Discoverly also sheds light on your email contacts regardless if you have been in contact with them before. It shows you all relevant info and social connections about email contacts. It’s similar to VibeApp in that you can view more data about your connections when viewing profiles across the web. For example, you can see your mutual Facebook friends on LinkedIn, or see Facebook and LinkedIn info on Twitter.

Discoverly(Free) | Availability Google Chrome

 Bottom Line

When it comes to the best alternative to Rapportive I would have to go with FullContact. This is due to the quality of data you get back and it is also useful to make notes on each contact. That said the data quality ofHubspots Sidekick is just as good as it appears it is powered by FullContact and it adds on a ton of features like tracking your email history with a contact which is really handy. It does seem that the search for a good replacement to Rapportive is now over with there being a lot of good options out there now.

If I failed to mention or if you have any feedback on the above feel free to comment below.
